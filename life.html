Life of Juice WRLD

From heartbreak to hip-hop stardom — the story of a legend gone too soon. Discover the life of Juice WRLD, a voice that changed a generation. Juice WRLD, born Jarad Higgins on December 2, 1998, in Chicago, Illinois, grew up surrounded by music. Raised by a single mother who was deeply religious, he was first introduced to rock and pop through video games and began experimenting with music during high school. His early tracks, posted on SoundCloud under the name JuiceTheKidd, quickly gained attention for their emotional honesty and unique blend of hip-hop, emo, and rock influences. He broke into the mainstream with the massive success of "Lucid Dreams" in 2018, a song that reflected on heartbreak and mental health — themes that would define much of his work. Signed to Interscope Records, Juice WRLD released several critically acclaimed projects, including Goodbye & Good Riddance, Death Race for Love, and the posthumous Legends Never Die. Despite his fame, he openly struggled with anxiety, depression, and substance abuse, often addressing these battles in his lyrics. Tragically, Juice WRLD died on December 8, 2019, after suffering a seizure at Chicago’s Midway Airport. He was only 21 years old. His music, however, continues to inspire millions, and his legacy lives on as a symbol of vulnerability, creativity, and the power of emotional expression in hip-hop.
